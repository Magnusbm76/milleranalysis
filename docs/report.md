## Source Books Overview

### "Do You Read Me" (2018)

**Publication Year:** 2018  
**Page Range in Analysis:** 28-95  
**Total Quotes:** 4 (Quote IDs: 001, 004, 007, 010)

**Overview:**
"Do You Read Me" explores the fundamental limitations of language and the semiotic gap between signifier and signified. The book draws heavily from Lacan's structuralist reinterpretation of Freud, examining how communication inevitably distorts meaning. Key themes include the failure of language, the silence within signifiers, historical context of psychoanalytic theory, and clinical applications of semiotic theory.

**Chapter Coverage:**
- Chapter 2: The Structural Turn (page 28)
- Chapter 3: The Semiotic Divide (page 42)
- Chapter 4: The Unspoken (page 63)
- Chapter 5: The Clinical Text (page 95)

**Thematic Focus:** Language & Semiotics, Philosophical Foundations, Clinical Applications

**Publication Year:** 2021  
**Page Range in Analysis:** 87-223  
**Total Quotes:** 8 (Quote IDs: 002, 003, 005, 006, 008, 009, 011, 012)
