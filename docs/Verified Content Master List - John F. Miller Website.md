### **Section: Key Concepts**

**NEW TEXT:** **The Triumphant Victim:** A psychological paradox where an individual adopts a stance of suffering to achieve a hidden moral superiority or control over others. Miller identifies this as an archetypal trap that prevents true healing by rewarding the ego for remaining in a state of woundedness.

**The Symbolic Journey:** The belief that every symptom is a message from the unconscious. Miller explores how the patient's narrative contains symbols of their potential growth, focusing on the teleological nature of the psyche—where it is trying to go, rather than just where it has been.

**The Analytic Encounter:** The transformational relationship between analyst and patient. Miller argues that the analyst must act as a 'container' for the patient's projections, allowing for the safe exploration of repressed material and the eventual integration of the self.

### **Section: FAQ**

NEW TEXT: Is this book suitable for clinical training?  
Yes. Do You Read Me? is designed as a foundational text for analysts in training, focusing on the psychological mechanics of the clinical encounter and the ethics of interpretation.  
How does this approach view victimhood?  
In The Triumphant Victim, Miller examines how 'the victim' can become a rigid identity or a 'complex' that halts personal development. He looks at how individuals can move from a state of 'triumphant suffering' to authentic agency.  
What is the 'Labyrinth' mentioned in the text?  
The Labyrinth is Miller's metaphor for the complex, inward path of the individuation process. It represents the twists and turns one must take to confront the various layers of the unconscious.
